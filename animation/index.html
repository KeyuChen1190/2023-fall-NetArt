In the land where rivers weave their tales,

Amongst trees standing proud and hale.

Leaves aflutter in the breeze's song,

A symphony of nature, where I belong.

Majestic mountains, silent guards,

Whispers carried by the zephyr's shards.

Leaves dance upon the windswept air,

A ballet of green, beyond compare.

A river's journey, a winding trail,

Reflects the moon, a silver sail.

Mountains echo ancient lore,

As leaves tell tales from days of yore.

Through bamboo groves and willow's weep,

The secrets of the land they keep.

Nature's canvas, strokes divine,

In every leaf, a story's sign.

Blowing winds, both fierce and kind,

Carry tales of hearts entwined.

In the rustling leaves, a gentle plea,

To cherish this landscape, wild and free.

So let the river serenade,

As leaves in the wind cascade.

In this realm where nature weaves,

A timeless dance among the leaves.
